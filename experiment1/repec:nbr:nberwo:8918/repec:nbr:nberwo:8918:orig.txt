27:670:53460:28:29:108:527:397:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4: As school districts impose tougher standards on students and increasingly hold schools 
27:671:53547:29:28:72:504:368:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:accountable for their performance, there will be a growing need to find effective remedial 
27:672:53638:28:28:72:504:340:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:education programs to help low-achieving students.  The evidence presented in this paper 
27:673:53727:28:29:72:507:312:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:suggests that summer school and grade retention have a modest but positive net impact on 
27:674:53816:29:28:72:533:283:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:achievement scores for third grade students, but little if any effect on academic achievement for 
27:675:53914:28:28:72:524:255:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:sixth grade students.  These findings are robust to various specifications, and do not appear to 
27:676:54011:28:28:72:533:227:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:vary substantially across different groups.   However, these programs do appear to have a larger 
27:677:54108:28:29:72:279:199:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:effect on math than reading achievement.   
27:678:54151:29:28:108:522:170:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4: For third grade students, the net effect is a combination of benefits from both summer 
27:679:54238:28:28:72:524:142:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:school and grade retention.  Contrary to conventional wisdom and prior research, we find that 
27:680:54332:28:29:72:539:114:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:retention may actually increase academic achievement for low-achieving third graders.  For sixth 
27:681:54429:29:0:72:529:85:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:grade students, the zero net effects appear to mask a small positive summer school effect and a 
28:682:54525:0:28:72:531:708:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:negative retention effect.  However, it is also possible that the negative effects for sixth graders 
28:683:54626:28:28:72:458:680:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:are driven by the higher incentives that promoted students face two years later.   
28:684:54709:28:29:108:524:652:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4: These finding suggest that school districts that implement policies aimed at increasing 
28:685:54797:29:28:72:539:623:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:standards and accountability can utilize summer school and (to a lesser extent) grade retention to 
28:686:54896:28:28:72:531:595:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:support students who cannot initially meet the new standards.  One limitation of this analysis is 
28:687:54994:28:29:72:513:567:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:that we were only able to follow students for several years following summer school and/or 
28:688:55085:29:28:72:531:538:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:retention.  It is possible that the summer school benefits will fade and that the long term effects 
28:689:55185:28:28:72:478:510:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:of retention will be worse than the short term effects.  As more time passes from the 
28:690:55271:28:29:72:541:482:144:g_d0_f4:144:g_d0_f4:implementation of the program, it will be possible to examine these possibilities in greater detail.
