2:30:1718:21:22:71:539:542:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:episodes of current account adjustment in industrial countries to assess the validity of these concerns.
2:31:1822:22:21:71:539:520:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:Our main findings are (i) larger deficits take longer to adjust and are associated with significantly

2:33:2022:22:21:71:539:477:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:(ii)  consumption-driven  current  account  deficits  involve  significantly  larger  depreciations  than
2:34:2127:21:22:71:539:456:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:deficits financing investment, and (iii) there is little evidence that deficits in economies that run
2:35:2228:22:21:71:539:434:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:persistent deficits, have large net foreign debt positions, experience greater short-term capital flows,
2:36:2332:21:22:71:539:413:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:or are less open are accommodated by more extensive exchange rate adjustment or slower growth.
2:37:2426:22:22:71:539:391:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:Our findings are consistent with earlier work showing that, in general, current account adjustment

2:39:2624:21:22:71:539:348:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:claims that the size of the current account deficit and the extent to which it is financing consumption

3:50:2929:28:28:125:522:681:144:Times:144:Times:The U.S. current account deficit was a record $668 billion in 2004, accounting for 
3:51:3012:28:27:89:520:653:144:Times:144:Times:5.7 percent of GDP and fully two-thirds of global net foreign lending.  Its size, as well as 
3:52:3105:27:28:89:518:626:144:Times:144:Times:the unprecedented foreign flows into U.S. bonds associated with it, have raised concerns

3:54:3279:27:28:89:512:571:144:Times:144:Times:scenario begins with foreigners suddenly losing their appetite for U.S. assets, and in the 
3:55:3370:28:28:89:499:543:144:Times:144:Times:process of unwinding their large U.S. positions, pushing up interest rates, depressing 
3:56:3457:28:27:89:511:515:144:Times:144:Times:growth, and causing a large depreciation of the dollar.  Worries about such a disorderly 
3:57:3546:27:28:89:515:488:144:Times:144:Times:adjustment first surfaced in 2000, when the U.S. deficit-GDP ratio crossed the 4 percent 
3:58:3635:28:27:89:126:460:144:Times:144:Times:mark.   
3:59:3643:27:28:125:498:433:144:Times:144:Times:The conventional wisdom on current account adjustment is that some current

3:61:3805:28:27:89:519:377:144:Times:144:Times:persistence of the deficit, its use and financing, and the openness and indebtedness of the 
3:62:3897:27:28:89:500:350:144:Times:144:Times:economy.  For example, Summers (2004) notes that 5 percent of GDP is a traditional 
3:63:3980:28:27:89:493:322:144:Times:144:Times:“danger point” for current account deficits, and argues that deficits rising to finance 
3:64:4068:27:28:89:524:295:144:Times:144:Times:consumption and government spending and deficits supported by short-term financing are

3:66:4243:28:27:89:475:239:144:Times:144:Times:goods market integration in adjustment because the magnitude of exchange rate 
3:67:4321:27:28:89:519:212:144:Times:144:Times:adjustment needed to reduce a deficit is greater when markets are not well integrated and 
3:68:4411:28:27:89:515:184:144:Times:144:Times:the substitution between foreign and domestic goods is low.  Roubini and Stetser (2005) 
3:69:4499:27:28:89:527:157:144:Times:144:Times:worry about the size of the foreign debt position and the corresponding interest payments.  
3:70:4591:28:0:89:471:129:144:Times:144:Times:Concerns about delaying a U.S. adjustment abound, for example, Bergsten and 
4:71:4669:0:28:89:511:709:144:Times:144:Times:Williamson (2004) write “[n]o one doubts that adjustment will eventually happen.  The 
4:72:4755:28:28:89:399:681:144:Times:144:Times:sooner it starts, the less chance it will take a catastrophic form.”

4:74:4897:27:28:89:498:626:144:Times:144:Times:situation within the context of current account reversals that have occurred in a wide 
4:75:4984:28:27:89:511:598:144:Times:144:Times:range of industrial countries.  In all, we have at our disposal twenty-six current account 
4:76:5075:27:28:89:489:571:144:Times:144:Times:reversals that occurred between 1980 and 2003.  The twenty-six episodes vary in a 
4:77:5157:28:28:89:523:543:144:Times:144:Times:number of ways and allow us to place the current U.S. situation in context; while the U.S. 
4:78:5248:28:27:89:498:515:144:Times:144:Times:may be in what it considers uncharted waters (with respect to its own history), along 
4:79:5334:27:28:89:356:488:144:Times:144:Times:many dimensions its current scenario is not atypical.

24:568:42720:0:28:89:187:709:144:Times:144:Times:Summary of Results 
24:569:42739:28:28:125:504:681:144:Times:144:Times: The results show that larger deficits are associated with slower income growth 
24:570:42818:28:27:89:521:653:144:Times:144:Times:during the current account recovery period and take somewhat longer to resolve.  Growth 
24:571:42906:27:28:89:506:626:144:Times:144:Times:effects are more severe because more adjustment is required when the current account 
24:572:42991:28:27:89:512:598:144:Times:144:Times:deficit is greater.  Indeed, as we have shown, growth (relative to long run) is negatively 
24:573:43082:27:28:89:480:571:144:Times:144:Times:correlated with the extent of adjustment (Figure 3).  Although deeper deficits are 
24:574:43165:28:28:89:523:543:144:Times:144:Times:associated with slower growth, they do not appear to require more depreciation.  Once we 
24:575:43254:28:27:89:504:515:144:Times:144:Times:control for other variables, exchange rate movements are not significantly different in 
24:576:43342:27:28:89:492:488:144:Times:144:Times:countries with deeper deficits.   In part, this may be because nominal exchange rate 
24:577:43427:28:27:89:506:460:144:Times:144:Times:adjustment is limited in some industrial countries, either because of managed systems, 
24:578:43514:27:28:89:495:433:144:Times:144:Times:fixed exchange rates, or because key trading partners fix exchange rates.  Restricted 
24:579:43600:28:28:89:494:405:144:Times:144:Times:exchange rate adjustment in turn leads to more extreme current account deficits and 
24:580:43684:28:27:89:493:377:144:Times:144:Times:lower income growth during current account recovery.   Income growth is forced to 
24:581:43766:27:28:89:499:350:144:Times:144:Times:accommodate adjustment precisely because depreciation is not more severe.  Indeed, 
24:582:43849:28:27:89:513:322:144:Times:144:Times:there is a strong inverse correlation between the extent of exchange rate adjustment and 
24:583:43938:27:28:89:517:295:144:Times:144:Times:the slowdown in GDP growth (Figure 4).  There is a tradeoff: adjustment comes through 
24:584:44024:28:28:89:510:267:144:Times:144:Times:either exchange movements or GDP growth.  If exchange rates movements are limited, 
24:585:44107:28:27:89:471:239:144:Times:144:Times:the current account position worsens further and the GDP hit is more extreme.  
24:586:44186:27:28:125:512:212:144:Times:144:Times: We also found that the resolution of persistent deficits and of deficits with large 
24:587:44270:28:27:89:489:184:144:Times:144:Times:negative NFA positions is broadly similar to others, in terms of total exchange rate 
24:588:44355:27:28:89:523:157:144:Times:144:Times:adjustment and growth effects.  Investment-driven current accounts require less exchange 
24:589:44444:28:28:89:498:129:144:Times:144:Times:rate adjustment than episodes driven by consumption or government spending.  This 
24:590:44526:28:0:89:517:101:144:Times:144:Times:implies that investment channels resources into exports which can eventually service the 
25:591:44618:0:28:89:507:709:144:Times:144:Times:debt.  Finally, we found that financing does not matter significantly for the adjustment 
25:592:44707:28:28:89:426:681:144:Times:144:Times:process, suggesting that markets are efficient at intermediating funds.

27:642:48690:13:28:89:163:640:144:Times:144:Times:V.  Conclusion 
27:643:48705:28:28:125:512:612:144:Times:144:Times: We have shown that large deficits are associated with a significant slowdown in 
27:644:48785:28:27:89:512:584:144:Times:144:Times:income growth, though if anything they involve less depreciation.  We think these facts 
27:645:48873:27:28:89:489:557:144:Times:144:Times:are related.  In countries where exchange movements are limited, either because of 
27:646:48956:28:27:89:507:529:144:Times:144:Times:managed systems, fixed exchange rates, or key partners fix exchange rates, the current 
27:647:49043:27:28:89:521:502:144:Times:144:Times:account will deteriorate more than if the exchange rate were flexible.  Moreover, because 
27:648:49133:28:28:89:491:474:144:Times:144:Times:of restricted exchange adjustment, growth will be forced to do much of the work of 
27:649:49216:28:27:89:504:446:144:Times:144:Times:adjustment.  Indeed, there is a very robust inverse correlation between income growth 
27:650:49302:27:28:89:382:419:144:Times:144:Times:and the total exchange rate adjustment during the recovery.   
27:651:49364:28:27:125:491:391:144:Times:144:Times: In contrast, persistent deficits do not lead to a more severe adjustment.  Our 
27:652:49443:27:28:89:480:364:144:Times:144:Times:results suggest that they may be slightly less disruptive in terms of exchange rate 
27:653:49527:28:28:89:516:336:144:Times:144:Times:movement, with depreciation beginning earlier in the episode and being somewhat more 
27:654:49612:28:27:89:509:308:144:Times:144:Times:limited.  In general, persistent-deficit countries are characterized by a low savings rate. 
27:655:49704:27:28:125:513:281:144:Times:144:Times: We also find that deficits driven by investment growth are more benign in terms 
27:656:49784:28:27:89:521:253:144:Times:144:Times:of exchange rate adjustment than deficits driven by consumption or fiscal spending.  This 
27:657:49874:27:28:89:498:226:144:Times:144:Times:is intuitive, since these are the economies where the accrued debt can be more easily 
27:658:49960:28:28:89:515:198:144:Times:144:Times:serviced.  There is only weak evidence that the level of openness reduces the magnitude 
27:659:50048:28:27:89:209:170:144:Times:144:Times:of exchange adjustment. 
27:660:50072:27:28:125:499:143:144:Times:144:Times: On the financing side, we find that the nature of the inflows while the current 
27:661:50152:28:27:89:521:115:144:Times:144:Times:account deficit is worsening does not impact the outcome.  That is, whether the financing 
27:662:50242:27:0:89:521:88:144:Times:144:Times:of the deficit comes through inflows of equity, direct investment, bonds, or bank deposits 
28:663:50336:0:28:89:519:709:144:Times:144:Times:has no apparent bearing on the adjustment process, possibly because financial systems in 
28:664:50425:28:28:89:517:681:144:Times:144:Times:industrial countries intermediate these flows rather well.   Finally, the size of the foreign 
28:665:50519:28:27:89:446:653:144:Times:144:Times:liabilities position seems to be uncorrelated with the adjustment process.
