2:27:1285:28:14:72:540:588:12:Times:12:Times:During the past few decades, the fraction of the equity market owned directly by individuals declined
2:28:1386:14:14:72:540:574:12:Times:12:Times:significantly.  The same period witnessed investment trends that include the growth of indexing as
2:29:1484:14:14:72:540:560:12:Times:12:Times:well as shifts by active managers toward lower fees and more index-like investing.  I develop an equilibrium
2:30:1592:14:14:72:540:546:12:Times:12:Times:model linking these investment trends to the decline in individual ownership, interpreting the latter
2:31:1693:14:14:72:540:532:12:Times:12:Times:as a reduction in noise trading.   Active management corrects most noise-trader induced mispricing,
2:32:1792:14:14:72:540:518:12:Times:12:Times:and the fraction left uncorrected shrinks as noise traders' stake in the market declines.  Less mispricing
2:33:1898:14:42:72:340:504:12:Times:12:Times:then dictates a smaller footprint for active management.

3:43:2288:18:18:72:539:680:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:in the stock market may have declined. Why? First, noise trading is more often associated with
3:44:2382:18:18:72:539:662:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:individuals than institutions. Fischer Black describes noise traders as “people who trade ...even
3:45:2479:18:18:72:540:644:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:though from an objective point of view they would be better off not trading.”1The behavioral
3:46:2571:18:18:71:539:626:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:finance literature points to numerous factors that lead individuals to make unproductive invest-
3:47:2667:18:18:71:540:608:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:ment decisions. Second, there has been a substantial downward trend in the fraction of U.S. equity

3:49:2865:18:18:71:539:572:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:than half, from 48% to around 20% based on estimates by French(2008), who also suggests the
3:50:2956:18:18:71:539:554:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:pool of investors most likely to make mistakes may have thus shrunk.2This decline in individual

3:52:3145:18:18:71:539:518:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:held more than 90% of U.S. corporate equity. Explanatory factors likely include government tax
3:53:3239:18:28:71:392:500:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:and retirement policies (Rydqvist, Spizman, and Strebulaev, 2014).

4:79:5614:29:18:89:540:651:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:I ask whether the above trends in investment management are consistent with the downward
4:80:5702:18:18:72:539:633:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:trend in individual equity ownership and an associated decline in noise trading. The basic hypoth-
4:81:5800:18:18:72:539:615:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:esis is that less noise trading implies a lower capacity for profitable active management. I explore
4:82:5900:18:18:72:539:597:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:the validity of this hypothesis by developing an equilibrium model for active management in a
4:83:5993:18:18:72:539:579:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:market with noise traders. The model is simple in many respects but captures the simultaneity be-
4:84:6090:18:18:72:540:561:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:tween the amount of mispricing and the amount of active management. I then calibrate the model
4:85:6184:18:17:72:539:543:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:and find it supports the consistency between the investment trends noted above and a decline in
4:86:6279:17:29:72:137:526:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:noise trading.
4:87:6293:29:18:89:540:497:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:The model implies that active management corrects most of the noise-trader induced mispricing
4:88:6386:18:18:72:540:479:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:that would otherwise exist. A fraction of the mispricing remains uncorrected, because active man-
4:89:6483:18:18:72:539:461:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:agers are impeded by both trading costs and idiosyncratic risk. The remaining mispricing allows
4:90:6578:18:18:72:540:443:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:active management to earn positive alpha at the expense of noise traders. The fraction of mispric-
4:91:6676:18:18:72:540:425:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:ing uncorrected by active management shrinks as the fraction of the equity market owned by noise
4:92:6772:18:51:72:520:407:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:traders declines. With less mispricing, active managementmust then have a smaller footprint.

24:1019:55466:52:42:242:369:212:17:g_d0_f3:17:g_d0_f3:IV.  Conclusions
24:1020:55482:42:17:89:540:170:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:In the equilibrium model developed and calibrated here, active management corrects most of
24:1021:55572:17:18:72:540:153:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:the noise-trader induced mispricing that would otherwise exist. A fraction of the mispricing re-
24:1022:55668:18:18:72:540:135:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:mains uncorrected, because active managers are impeded by both risk and trading costs.  The
24:1023:55759:18:18:72:539:117:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:remaining mispricing allows investors who allocate a portion of their wealth to active management
24:1024:55856:18:18:72:540:99:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:to profit (earn alpha) at the expense of noise traders. The fraction of mispricing that remains uncor-
24:1025:55958:18:0:72:539:81:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:rected by active management shrinks as the fraction of the equity market owned by noise traders
25:1026:56055:0:18:72:540:716:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:declines. The decline in noise trading thus dictates a smaller footprint for active management. That
25:1027:56155:18:18:72:540:698:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:smaller footprint can involve lower fees and lower trackingerror, or a lower allocation by investors
25:1028:56255:18:18:72:540:680:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:to active management, or a combination of those effects. Forindividual active managers as well as
25:1029:56352:18:18:72:540:662:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:their overall industry, the fee revenue collected is invariant to the fee rate; a higher fee rate simply
25:1030:56456:18:18:72:540:644:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:means that investors allocate correspondingly less money to be managed. Both the alpha and the
25:1031:56550:18:18:72:540:626:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:market-adjusted volatility of the aggregate active portfolio are proportional to the fee rate, which
25:1032:56650:18:29:72:337:608:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:therefore drops out of that portfolio’s information ratio.
25:1033:56708:29:18:89:540:579:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:Active management in recent decades experienced downward trends in (i) market share, (ii) fee
25:1034:56801:18:18:72:540:561:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:rates, (iii) tracking error, and (iv) deviations from benchmark stock weightings (active share). The
25:1035:56901:18:17:72:540:543:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:model contains two unknown parameters in addition to those that have straightforward empirical
25:1036:56995:17:18:72:540:526:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:counterparts. The first parameter governs trading costs while the second parameter characterizes
25:1037:57091:18:18:72:540:508:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:the extent to which the low diversification typical of individual investors survives aggregation.
25:1038:57188:18:18:72:540:490:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:These two parameters are calibrated using mid-sample (1996) levels for the fraction of individual
25:1039:57285:18:18:72:539:472:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:equity ownership, the market share of active management, the active fee rate, and tracking error.
25:1040:57382:18:18:72:539:454:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:Implied values for the other years in the 1980–2012 sample period reveal that the downward trends
25:1041:57479:18:18:72:539:436:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:in active management are mutually consistent with a declinein the fraction of the market owned by
25:1042:57576:18:18:72:540:418:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:individual investors, viewed here as noise traders. If individual ownership and thus noise trading
25:1043:57674:18:18:72:539:400:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:continue to decline, the model implies that both fee revenueand tracking error must decline as
25:1044:57768:18:18:72:540:382:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:well, just to keep active management’s market share from declining more rapidly in the future than
25:1045:57866:18:28:72:242:364:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:it did during the 1980–2012 period.
25:1046:57901:28:18:89:539:336:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:It is well known that active management in aggregate has historically under-performed passive
25:1047:57994:18:18:72:540:318:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:benchmarks. If investors unwittingly allocate more to active management than is justified by the
25:1048:58090:18:18:72:540:300:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:current fee rate and degree of noise-trader mispricing, then the resulting alpha on active manage-
25:1049:58188:18:18:72:539:282:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:ment can become negative. In other words, the active management industry in the model exhibits
25:1050:58282:18:18:72:540:264:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:decreasing returns to scale. If investors must arrive at theappropriate investment level by infer-
25:1051:58380:18:18:72:540:246:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:ring empirically the effect of scale on performance, adjustment to the correct level of investment
25:1052:58478:18:18:72:539:228:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:can be slow, as shown by P ́astor and Stambaugh (2012). A prescription that all investors react
25:1053:58573:18:18:72:540:210:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:to the historical under-performance by simply reallocating everything to passive investing would
25:1054:58669:18:18:72:539:192:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:be ill-advised, however, at least in the context of the model. Unless there is zero noise trading,
25:1055:58767:18:18:72:540:174:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:the result would leave an unexploited positive alpha for potential active investing as well as fully
25:1056:58867:18:18:72:539:156:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:uncorrected noise-trader induced mispricing. The latter would presumably impose a significant
25:1057:58960:18:29:72:347:138:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:negative externality arising from allocational inefficiency.
25:1058:59020:29:18:89:540:109:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:The simple model presented here abstracts from potentiallyimportant considerations in addi-
25:1059:59111:18:0:72:540:91:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:tion to the retail-versus-institutional segmentation discussed earlier. Incorporating heterogeneity
26:1060:59212:0:18:72:539:716:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:in skill and information across investment managers is likely a potentially important extension.
26:1061:59308:18:18:72:539:698:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:Also, the current model takes the information possessed by active managers as exogenous. An-
26:1062:59400:18:18:72:539:680:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:other worthwhile extension would be to make information acquisition endogenous, possibly an
26:1063:59491:18:0:72:500:662:12:g_d0_f1:12:g_d0_f1:increasing function of fee revenue. These and other issues leave much for future research.
