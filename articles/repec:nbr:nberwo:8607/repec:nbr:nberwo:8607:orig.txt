3:53:3344:18:18:72:540:608:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:production technologies, influence asset prices.  As shown in a recent paper by Campbell
3:54:3432:18:18:72:540:590:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:and Cochrane (1999), many of the empirically observed features of stock prices can be re-

3:57:3708:18:18:72:540:536:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:premium in stock returns.  In this paper we explore a specific economic mechanism leading
3:58:3797:18:18:72:540:518:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:to countercyclical variation in the conditional risk premium.  We study an economy popu-

3:62:4161:18:18:72:540:446:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:risk tolerant agents hold higher proportion of their wealth in stocks.  Therefore, a decline

3:66:4478:18:18:89:539:374:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:We calibrate our model to match the basic unconditional moments of stock and bond re-

3:72:5019:18:18:72:540:265:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:risk preferences can give rise to many of the observed properties of asset returns, the simple
3:73:5113:18:18:72:540:247:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:structure of our model does not capture all of the important quantitative features of the

18:687:43720:29:18:72:540:681:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:Representative agent models identify economic mechanisms that generate empirically ob-
18:688:43806:18:19:72:540:663:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:served  features of asset  prices.   One such  mechanism  is countercyclical  variation in  the
18:689:43901:19:18:72:540:644:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:aggregate risk aversion and the market price of risk, as shown by Campbell and Cochrane
18:690:43988:18:18:72:540:626:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:(1999). This effect arises in our model as a result of heterogeneity in risk preferences among
18:691:44082:18:18:72:540:608:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:the agents.  In a heterogeneous economy, moves in the stock market trigger changes in the
18:692:44171:18:18:72:540:590:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:cross-sectional distribution of wealth, which we show to cause countercyclical variation in
18:693:44262:18:18:71:379:572:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:the conditional risk premium and volatility of stock returns.
18:694:44323:18:18:89:540:554:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:While endogenous changes in aggregate risk aversion contribute with the right sign to the
18:695:44412:18:18:71:539:536:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:pattern of time variation in expected returns and volatility, a nontrivial fraction of expected
18:696:44507:18:18:71:539:518:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:return variation in our model is still due to changes in the risk-free rate.  This property of
18:697:44601:18:18:71:539:500:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:the model is explained by our choice of the specific functional form of preferences. Thus, we
18:698:44694:18:18:71:539:482:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:cannot argue that the observed empirical properties of stock returns should be attributed
18:699:44783:18:18:71:540:464:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:to heterogeneous risk preferences alone. Nevertheless, the ability of our heterogeneous-agent
18:700:44876:18:18:71:539:446:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:model to replicate various qualitative features of aggregate stock returns is encouraging. It
18:701:44969:18:18:71:539:428:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:suggests that many salient features of the data can arise naturally as a result of interaction
18:702:45063:18:0:71:331:410:143:g_d0_f4:143:g_d0_f4:of rational investors with different risk preferences.
