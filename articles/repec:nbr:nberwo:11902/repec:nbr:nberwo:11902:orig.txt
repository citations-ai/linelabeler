3:45:2268:14:28:107:530:681:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4: Common sense, backed by a good deal of evidence, suggests that drinking could impair 
3:46:2353:28:28:71:516:653:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:productivity.  In fact, estimates of alcohol-related social costs are typically dominated by the 
3:47:2450:28:28:71:536:625:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:value of lost productivity (Harwood, Fountain & Livermore 1998). Historically the concern with 
3:48:2545:28:28:71:520:597:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:the quality and quantity of work provided by the labor force was a major factor in Nineteenth 
3:49:2639:28:28:71:515:569:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:Century temperance movements in the United States and Europe (Roberts 1984; Rumbarger 
3:50:2725:28:27:71:375:541:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:1989).  Clark Warburton (1932) stated the argument concisely: 
3:51:2787:27:21:107:536:514:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4: Prohibition, if it actually resulted in the cessation of use of alcoholic beverages, might be 
3:52:2881:21:21:107:511:493:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:expected to affect the efficiency of industry in several ways.  The principal effect of 
3:53:2969:21:21:107:518:472:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:alcohol is on the central nervous system, and experiments show that a decrease in the 
3:54:3055:21:21:107:539:451:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:consumption of alcohol during, or immediately preceding, working hours is accompanied 
3:55:3141:21:21:107:537:430:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:by greater skill at work.  The finer co-ordination made possible by the absence of alcohol 
3:56:3232:21:21:107:506:409:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:tends to reduce the accident rate.  The elimination of drinking bouts should tend to 
3:57:3317:21:21:107:533:388:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:eliminate absenteeism, especially on Monday, and irregularity in reporting at work.  The 
3:58:3406:21:20:107:533:367:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:impossibility of drinking to excess should result in less sickness and absence on account 
3:59:3496:20:21:107:377:347:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:thereof, and in a longer average working life (195-196). 
3:60:3553:21:14:71:74:326:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4: 
3:61:3553:14:28:71:478:312:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:In recent times a majority of large corporations in the United States have established 
3:62:3640:28:28:71:518:284:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:occupational alcoholism programs or employee-assistance programs to improve productivity 
3:63:3729:28:28:71:149:256:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:(Walsh 1982).   
3:64:3745:28:28:107:536:228:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4: Curiously, however, the link between drinking and reduced productivity (as measured by 
3:65:3832:28:27:71:527:200:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:earnings) does not receive unambiguous support from the econometric work on this subject. In 
3:66:3925:27:28:71:516:173:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:fact, drinkers as a group earn more than those who abstain, and that “drinker’s bonus” is not 
3:67:4019:28:0:71:539:145:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:necessarily limited to the moderate drinkers (Berger & Leigh 1988; Cook 1991; French & Zarkin 
4:68:4114:0:27:71:538:708:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:1995; Heien 1996; Hamilton & Hamilton 1997; Zarkin, et al. 1998; MacDonald & Shields 2001; 
4:69:4205:27:28:71:524:681:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:van Ours 2004).  These findings are intriguing, and have generated speculation about possible 
4:70:4299:28:28:71:517:653:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:mechanisms.  Perhaps drinking confers health benefits, or enhances “networking.”  But such 
4:71:4390:28:28:71:509:625:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:speculations presume that the statistical association is the result, at least in part, of a causal 
4:72:4489:28:28:71:525:597:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:process flowing from drinking to earnings.  A more obvious interpretation is just the reverse – 
4:73:4585:28:28:71:531:569:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:that earnings influence whether and how much someone drinks.  Indeed, there is good evidence 
4:74:4678:28:27:71:518:541:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:that ethanol is a normal commodity, with consumption increasing with income (Ruhm 1995; 
4:75:4766:27:28:71:196:514:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:Cook & Tauchen 1982).   
4:76:4790:28:28:107:516:486:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4: A number of analysts have considered the possibility of two-way causation, typically 
4:77:4875:28:28:71:530:458:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:using instrumental variables (IV) methods to remove any influence of earnings on drinking and 
4:78:4969:28:28:71:515:430:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:isolate the putative effect of drinking on earnings.  Those studies, reviewed below, have had 
4:79:5063:28:28:71:494:402:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:mixed results, in part because of the questionable specifications used in some of them.   
4:80:5153:28:27:107:533:374:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4: In this paper we estimate a series of earnings equations for youthful (age 21-35) workers 
4:81:5243:27:28:71:519:347:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:based on data from the National Longitudinal Survey of Youth (1979 cohort).  As have other 
4:82:5334:28:28:71:524:319:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:analysts, we are able to document the existence of the drinker’s bonus in these data, both with 
4:83:5430:28:28:71:534:291:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:and without a long list of controls for personal and labor-market characteristics.  We then report 
4:84:5529:28:28:71:531:263:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:the results of a reduced-form analysis using a new index of alcohol-excise taxes, demonstrating 
4:85:5625:28:28:71:534:235:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:that the prevalence of full-time work increases with the state tax level. We conclude that at least 
4:86:5725:28:28:71:521:207:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:for this population (adults prior to middle age) drinking does not improve the quality of labor 
4:87:5821:28:27:71:539:179:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:supply, and may well reduce the quantity.  The drinker’s bonus is most likely an artifact resulting 
4:88:5921:27:0:71:517:152:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:from the reverse causal process; quite simply, drinking is a normal activity, the likelihood of 
5:89:6018:0:27:71:230:708:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:which increases with earnings.    
5:90:6052:27:28:107:540:681:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4: Also of possible interest are our ancillary findings concerning individual characteristics --

5:102:6907:27:28:107:520:347:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4: An early effort to estimate the productivity costs of drinking is due to Yale economist 
5:103:6995:28:28:71:526:319:12:g_d0_f4:12:g_d0_f4:Irving Fisher (1926).  His view was that drinking slowed down the "human machine" (p. 118),
