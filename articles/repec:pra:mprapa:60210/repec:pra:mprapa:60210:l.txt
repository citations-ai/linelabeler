3:52:2747:21:20:70:524:477:12:Times:12:Times:B:evidence  to  the  existing  literature  on  the  topic  by  comparing  the  effect  of  two  kinds  of 
3:53:2851:20:21:70:524:457:12:Times:12:Times:B:volunteering on  health  across  14  European  countries:  we  study  in  depth  the  correlation  of 
3:54:2952:21:21:70:524:436:12:Times:12:Times:B:formal  and  informal  volunteering  with  health.  Informal  volunteering  consists  in  voluntary 
3:55:3052:21:21:70:524:415:12:Times:12:Times:B:activities  (performed  on  an  individual  basis)  to  help  someone  (such  as  cooking  for  others, 
3:56:3156:21:20:70:524:394:12:Times:12:Times:B:taking  care  of  people  in  hospitals/at  home)  while  formal  volunteering  consists  in  voluntary 
3:57:3260:20:21:70:524:374:12:Times:12:Times:B:activities  undertaken  in  charitable  organizations, groups  or  clubs.  Second,  to  the  best  of  our 
3:58:3367:21:21:70:524:353:12:Times:12:Times:B:knowledge, there are no economic studies which consider the impact of informal volunteering 
3:59:3459:21:20:70:120:332:12:Times:12:Times:B:on health.

3:67:4087:45:20:85:234:143:12:Times:12:Times:A:II. Volunteering and health 
3:68:4114:20:21:85:524:123:12:Times:12:Times:A:There  are  many  benefits  to  formal  and  informal  volunteering  for  volunteers.  People,  who 
3:69:4214:21:20:70:524:102:12:Times:12:Times:B:formally  volunteer,  get  work  experience  which,  in  turn,  raises  their  future  employability, 
3:70:4316:20:0:70:524:82:12:Times:12:Times:B:when unemployed, and earning power, when employed (Menchik and Weisbrod, 1987; Bruno 
4:71:4403:0:20:70:524:745:12:Times:12:Times:B:and Fiorillo, 2014).  In addition, since formal volunteering is  an activity  generally performed 
4:72:4501:20:21:70:524:725:12:Times:12:Times:B:in  a  group,  it  is  a  way  to  make  friends  (Clotfelter,  1985;  Prouteau  and  Wolff,  2004,  2006; 
4:73:4608:21:21:70:527:704:12:Times:12:Times:B:Schiff,  1990),  to  expand  one's  personal  network,  and  to  improve  social  skills.  Furthermore, 
4:74:4712:21:20:70:524:683:12:Times:12:Times:B:volunteering  may  contribute  to  make  volunteers  feel  «good»  (Andreoni,  1990).  In  this  case, 
4:75:4815:20:21:70:524:663:12:Times:12:Times:B:volunteering  is  an  ordinary  consumption  good  (Menchik  and  Weisbrod,  1987),  and  gives 
4:76:4911:21:21:70:524:642:12:Times:12:Times:B:people the opportunity to be recognized as «good» by society. Lastly, a growing strand of the 
4:77:5005:21:20:70:527:621:12:Times:12:Times:B:socio-medical  literature  has  focused  on  the  possibility  that  volunteering  is  good  for  health 
4:78:5110:20:21:70:530:601:12:Times:12:Times:B:(Casiday et al., 2008; Kumar et al., 2012; Musick and Wilson, 2003; Piliavin and Siegel, 2007; 
4:79:5205:21:21:70:135:580:12:Times:12:Times:B:Tang, 2009). 
4:80:5218:21:21:85:523:559:12:Times:12:Times:A:Contrary  to  formal  volunteering,  informal  volunteering  is  an  unpaid  activity,  likely 
4:81:5313:21:20:70:524:538:12:Times:12:Times:B:performed for purely altruistic reasons, since it is not performed via official groups but on an 
4:82:5410:20:21:70:527:518:12:Times:12:Times:B:individual  basis.  However,  it  seems  reasonable  that  also  informal  volunteering  may  confer 
4:83:5511:21:21:70:524:497:12:Times:12:Times:B:some  of  the  same  benefits  associated  to  formal  volunteering  (albeit  to  a  lesser  extent).  For 
4:84:5618:21:20:70:523:476:12:Times:12:Times:B:example,  also  helping  people  on  an  individual  basis  may  indirectly  and  inevitably  yield  a 
4:85:5721:20:21:70:527:456:12:Times:12:Times:B:potential  result  in  terms  of  human  capital  accumulation.  Also,  informal  volunteering  means 
4:86:5823:21:21:70:524:435:12:Times:12:Times:B:interactions  among  individuals  (probably  within  smaller  groups  compared  with  formal 
4:87:5916:21:20:70:461:414:12:Times:12:Times:B:volunteering), with the opportunity to make friends and to improve social skills.  
4:88:5999:20:21:85:527:394:12:Times:12:Times:A:Potential channels through which volunteering benefits health may work all simultaneously, 
4:89:6090:21:21:70:524:373:12:Times:12:Times:B:in partial combination or each on its own. This is likely to depend also on the characteristics 
4:90:6186:21:20:70:331:352:12:Times:12:Times:B:of the activity in question, which entail the following: 
4:91:6243:20:21:85:524:332:12:Times:12:Times:A:1) Self-esteem,  self-efficacy.  Whilst  performing  social  roles  connected  to  volunteering, 
4:92:6340:21:21:70:524:311:12:Times:12:Times:B:volunteers  may  be  distracted  from  personal  problems  and  become  less  self-preoccupied,  fill 
4:93:6442:21:21:70:527:290:12:Times:12:Times:B:their life with meaning and purpose, and expand social interactions. All this, in turn, produces 
4:94:6539:21:20:70:524:269:12:Times:12:Times:B:positive effects on socio-psychological factors (Musick and Wilson, 2003; Choi and Bohman, 
4:95:6630:20:21:70:107:249:12:Times:12:Times:B:2007).  
4:96:6638:21:21:85:524:228:12:Times:12:Times:A:2) Reciprocity. Reciprocity can be defined as a situation in which individuals are involved 
4:97:6730:21:20:70:524:207:12:Times:12:Times:B:in mutual exchanges, based not on obligations linked to a contract, but on the willingness to 
4:98:6824:20:21:70:524:187:12:Times:12:Times:B:build  and  to  reinforce  a  social  network  of  cooperation  (Zamagni,  1998).  “Doing  good”  for 
4:99:6926:21:21:70:524:166:12:Times:12:Times:B:others  develops  trust  among  people,  which,  in  turn,  produces  a  feeling  of  security  and 
4:100:7026:21:20:70:492:145:12:Times:12:Times:B:reciprocal acceptance among volunteers and those who receive their help (Post, 2005).  
4:101:7113:20:21:85:524:125:12:Times:12:Times:A:3) “Buffering effect”. Volunteering provides moral and affective support, which mitigates 
4:102:7203:21:0:70:524:104:12:Times:12:Times:B:psychological  distress  related  to  sickness  (Lin  et al,  1999).  Moreover,  expanded  social 
5:103:7303:0:20:70:523:745:12:Times:12:Times:B:contacts and improvements in self-confidence, coming from volunteering, are likely to buffer 
5:104:7396:20:21:70:235:725:12:Times:12:Times:B:stress and lessen risks of disease.  
5:105:7433:21:21:85:527:704:12:Times:12:Times:A:4) Reputation.  Since  society  appreciates  volunteering  activities,  volunteers  may  enhance 
5:106:7530:21:20:70:348:683:12:Times:12:Times:B:feelings of self-worth which, in turn, may benefit health.  
5:107:7590:20:21:85:524:663:12:Times:12:Times:A:5) Social  norms.  Volunteering  may  foster  the  development  of  social  norms  that  support 
5:108:7687:21:21:70:524:642:12:Times:12:Times:B:health-promoting  behaviors,  such  as  prevention  and physical  activity,  or  may  constrain 
5:109:7783:21:20:70:307:621:12:Times:12:Times:B:unhealthy habits, such as drinking and smoking.  
5:110:7832:20:21:85:524:601:12:Times:12:Times:A:Volunteering  benefits  seem  to  be  stronger  for  elderly  people.  As  suggested  by  activity 
5:111:7931:21:21:70:524:580:12:Times:12:Times:B:theory  (Lemon  et  al.,  1972;  Kart  and  Longino,  1982),  keeping  active  and  sharing  social 
5:112:8031:21:21:70:524:559:12:Times:12:Times:B:relationships  in  old  age  is  good  for  health  because  it  protects  the  elderly  from  isolation  in 
5:113:8140:21:20:70:527:538:12:Times:12:Times:B:difficult  periods.  Furthermore,  since  volunteering allows  people  to  be  active  and  productive 
5:114:8243:20:21:70:523:518:12:Times:12:Times:B:and to gain self-esteem, such activity can be considered a good substitute for paid work when 
5:115:8337:21:21:70:524:497:12:Times:12:Times:B:people retire (Midlarsky, 1991). This has a positive impact on health particularly in a society 
5:116:8432:21:20:70:527:476:12:Times:12:Times:B:where  the  transition  from  work  to  retirement  is  not  easy,  since  being  useful  is  everybody's 
5:117:8538:20:45:70:115:456:12:Times:12:Times:B:priority.
