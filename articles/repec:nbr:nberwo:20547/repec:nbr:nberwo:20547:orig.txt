26:1213:61422:27:14:72:540:698:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:This paper introduces a parsimonious dynamic model of retiree behavior featuring uninsured
26:1214:61512:14:14:72:539:684:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:health expenditure risks and household portfolios consisting of annuities and liquid wealth.
26:1215:61604:14:15:72:540:670:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:The model incorporates sufficient detail to allow interesting calibrations, yet it remains
26:1216:61694:15:14:72:540:655:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:tractable and able to provide intuitive explanations for post-retirement household behavior.
26:1217:61786:14:15:72:539:641:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:The model delivers a coherent framework for interpreting three key facts on retireefinancial
26:1218:61878:15:14:72:540:626:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:decisions: (i) lack of decumulation of liquid assets after retirement, (ii) low demand for
26:1219:61968:14:15:72:533:612:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:secondary annuities (i.e., the “annuity puzzle”), and (iii) relatively infrequent of bequests.
26:1220:62062:15:14:89:540:597:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:The model’s explanation for thefirst fact derives from cohort composition by health status
26:1221:62152:14:15:72:539:583:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:and substantial pre-annuitized wealth of households entering retirement. In particular, the
26:1222:62243:15:14:72:540:568:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:distribution of households by health status becomes asymptotically stationary, making the
26:1223:62332:14:14:72:540:554:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:behavior of healthy households pivotal in determining the cohort average wealth trajectories.
26:1224:62425:14:15:72:539:540:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:Wefind that households enjoying high health status may often desire to add to their liquid
26:1225:62515:15:14:72:540:525:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:assets after retirement. And, surprisingly, their willingness to do so tends to increase with
26:1226:62608:14:15:72:253:511:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:their holdings of annuitized wealth.
26:1227:62644:15:14:89:539:496:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:The limited demand for market annuities can be partially explained by their inflexibility
26:1228:62733:14:15:72:540:482:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:and, for secondary annuities, in particular, high transactions costs. Households choosing
26:1229:62822:15:14:72:540:467:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:secondary annuities face a trade-offbetween inflexible incomeflow and a return that exceeds
26:1230:62913:14:15:72:540:453:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:the interest rate on bonds. Our numerical analysis shows that when annuity loads exceed
26:1231:63000:15:14:72:429:438:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:30%, household demand for secondary annuities tends to be very low.
26:1232:63067:14:15:89:539:424:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:We also put forward a less conventional explanation for the annuity puzzle. We argue
26:1233:63151:15:14:72:540:409:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:that non-market annuities for thefinal stage of life may be important in practice (and they
26:1234:63242:14:14:71:539:395:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:may be an attractive substitute for long-term care insurance). When household portfolio
26:1235:63329:14:15:71:539:381:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:options include standard annuities at retirement, non-market annuities at the last stage
26:1236:63417:15:14:71:540:366:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:of life, and liquid assets at all ages, wefind that non-market annuities tend to dominate
26:1237:63506:14:15:71:344:352:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:standard annuities (even if the latter have zero load).

26:1239:63648:14:15:71:539:323:145:g_d0_f1:145:g_d0_f1:thin annuitization with the relative paucity of bequests observed in inheritance surveys.
