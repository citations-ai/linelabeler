4:79:4297:24:13:108:513:688:10:Courier:10:Courier:In an open economy, either the money supply or the nominal exchange
4:80:4364:13:12:77:526:675:10:Courier:10:Courier:rate can serve as a nominal anchor.  Such an anchor  is usually viewed as a
4:81:4439:12:12:77:532:663:10:Courier:10:Courier:necessary condition for macroeconomic  stability since, at least in the long
4:82:4515:12:12:78:520:651:10:Courier:10:Courier:run, all nominal variables will converge  to the pre-set rate of growth of
4:83:4589:12:13:78:514:639:10:Courier:10:Courier:either the money supply or the exchange rate.  Assuming that appropriate
4:84:4661:13:12:78:532:626:10:Courier:10:Courier:fiscal and microeconomic policies are in place, the price stability brought
4:85:4736:12:12:78:527:614:10:Courier:10:Courier:about by a nominal anchor should ensure that the economy achieves long-run
4:86:4810:12:25:78:175:602:10:Courier:10:Courier:economic growth.
4:87:4826:25:12:109:520:577:10:Courier:10:Courier:In practice, however, such a "neo-classical" view of policymaking  is
4:88:4895:12:12:78:526:565:10:Courier:10:Courier:not universally accepted.  Policymakers  are not always content, especially
4:89:4970:12:13:79:539:553:10:Courier:10:Courier:in the short-run, with targeting nominal variables such as the exchange rate
4:90:5046:13:12:79:496:540:10:Courier:10:Courier:or the money supply.  After all, the reasoning goes, if one is really
4:91:5115:12:12:79:533:528:10:Courier:10:Courier:interested  in the behavior of real variables which have a direct bearing on
4:92:5191:12:12:79:509:516:10:Courier:10:Courier:growth  (such as the real exchange rate or real interest rates), why not
4:93:5263:12:12:78:496:504:10:Courier:10:Courier:bypass nominal targets and simply try to set those variables at their
4:94:5332:12:12:79:498:492:10:Courier:10:Courier:desired  levels?  Thus, in spite of dire warnings from neo-classically
4:95:5402:12:12:80:479:480:10:Courier:10:Courier:inclined economists about the perils of losing the nominal anchor,
4:96:5468:12:12:79:528:468:10:Courier:10:Courier:policymakers have time and time again pursued real targets  in implementing
4:97:5543:12:25:79:285:456:10:Courier:10:Courier:monetary  and exchange rate policy.
4:98:5578:25:13:110:498:431:10:Courier:10:Courier:Since  the real exchange rate  is a key relative price in any open
4:99:5644:13:12:80:538:418:10:Courier:10:Courier:economy,  it is probably  the most popular real target t at least  in developing
4:100:5724:12:12:80:486:406:10:Courier:10:Courier:countries. 1/  By definition, then, a policy of "real exchange rate
4:101:5791:12:12:80:528:394:10:Courier:10:Courier:targeting" aims at controlling  the level of the real exchange rate, either
4:102:5866:12:13:81:504:382:10:Courier:10:Courier:in an effort  to keep  it at a constant level  in the face of domestic or
4:103:5939:13:24:80:522:369:10:Courier:10:Courier:external shocks, or achieve a different  (usually more depreciated) level.
4:104:6013:24:12:111:541:345:10:Courier:10:Courier:Diverse circumstances  lead policymakers  to engage  in real exchange  rate
4:105:6088:12:12:81:498:333:10:Courier:10:Courier:targeting.  One of the most common reasons  is the desire  to index the
4:106:6159:12:13:80:462:321:10:Courier:10:Courier:nominal rate  to the price  level in an effort  to avoid losses  in
4:107:6226:13:12:81:541:308:10:Courier:10:Courier:competitiveness.  Such policies are usually referred  to as "purchasing power

4:110:6453:12:12:81:517:272:10:Courier:10:Courier:exchange  rate was changed by small percentages  at irregular  intervals of

4:112:6602:12:12:81:541:248:10:Courier:10:Courier:Brazil  and the United States.  With occasional deviations  (due, for example,
