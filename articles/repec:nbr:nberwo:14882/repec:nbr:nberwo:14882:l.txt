2:28:1435:28:14:72:540:588:12:Times:12:Times:C:A large sample approximation of the posterior distribution of partially identified structural parameters

2:30:1638:14:14:72:540:560:12:Times:12:Times:C:is used to analyze the differences between frequentist confidence sets and Bayesian credible sets in
2:31:1738:14:14:72:540:546:12:Times:12:Times:C:partially identified models. A key difference is that frequentist set estimates extend beyond the boundaries
2:32:1846:14:14:72:540:532:12:Times:12:Times:C:of the identified set (conditional on the estimated reduced form parameter), whereas Bayesian credible
2:33:1948:14:14:72:540:518:12:Times:12:Times:C:sets can asymptotically be located in the interior of the identified set. Our asymptotic approximations
2:34:2051:14:14:72:540:504:12:Times:12:Times:C:are illustrated in the context of simple moment inequality models and a numerical illustration for a
2:35:2151:14:42:72:240:490:12:Times:12:Times:C:two-player entry game is provided.

3:56:2830:18:18:110:506:569:10:g_d0_f2:10:g_d0_f2:B:problem has traditionally be addressed by imposing enough restrictions on the structural

3:61:3273:18:18:110:506:479:10:g_d0_f2:10:g_d0_f2:B:partially identified structural VAR. In DSGE models partial identification arises for instance
3:62:3367:18:18:110:506:461:10:g_d0_f2:10:g_d0_f2:B:if a subset of structural parameters guarantees the uniqueness of a rational expectations
3:63:3456:18:18:110:506:443:10:g_d0_f2:10:g_d0_f2:B:equilibrium but does not affect the equilibrium law of motion, e.g., Lubik and Schorfheide
3:64:3546:18:22:110:140:425:10:g_d0_f2:10:g_d0_f2:B:(2004).
3:65:3553:22:18:128:506:403:10:g_d0_f2:10:g_d0_f2:A:Partially-identified models also percolate the microeconometric literature and include
3:66:3639:18:18:110:506:385:10:g_d0_f2:10:g_d0_f2:B:censored sampling models and models for interval data, surveyed at length in Manski (2003).
3:67:3730:18:18:110:506:367:10:g_d0_f2:10:g_d0_f2:B:Partial identification arises in models of industrial organization, for instance, in game-

3:69:3906:18:17:110:506:331:10:g_d0_f2:10:g_d0_f2:B:(1994), Halie and Tamer (2003), Pakes, Porter, Ho, and Ishi (2005), Bajari, Benkard, and
3:70:3994:17:18:110:506:314:10:g_d0_f2:10:g_d0_f2:B:Levin (2007), and Ciliberto and Tamer (2007). Given the lack of point identification re-
