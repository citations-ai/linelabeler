3:59:3789:20:21:90:510:502:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:83.1 billion dollars (Greenberg et al. 2003).  This figure is driven largely by workplace 
3:60:3879:21:21:90:493:481:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:costs, which were estimated to be 51.5 billion dollars.  Recent findings suggest that 
3:61:3965:21:21:90:486:460:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:employees treated for depression incur annual health and disability costs of nearly 
3:62:4049:21:20:90:505:439:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:$5,500, which is significantly more than costs for other illnesses such as hypertension

3:64:4224:21:21:90:514:398:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:2000).  However, most of these estimates may be partly attributable to environment and 
3:65:4310:21:20:90:509:377:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:family-level confounding factors as well as issues of reverse causality, which may bias 
3:66:4398:20:21:90:515:357:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:upward typical estimates. With such substantial potential costs attached to depression, it 
3:67:4489:21:21:90:514:336:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:is important to further understand how it is linked to labor force outcomes, among other 
3:68:4578:21:20:90:479:315:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:outcomes, by leveraging new data and research designs not typically used in this 
3:69:4659:20:21:90:523:295:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:literature.   Also needed is an examination of the potential longer term costs of adolescent 
3:70:4752:21:21:90:365:274:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:B:depression that have not been explored in the literature.
