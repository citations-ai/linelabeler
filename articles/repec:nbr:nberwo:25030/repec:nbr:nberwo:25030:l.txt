2:26:1165:28:14:72:546:588:12:Times:12:Times:C:Evidence   from   different   sources   shows   that   spouses'   retirement   decisions   are   correlated. 
2:27:1274:14:14:72:545:574:12:Times:12:Times:C:Retirement policies affecting individuals in couples are therefore also likely to affect behavior of 
2:28:1374:14:14:72:546:560:12:Times:12:Times:C:their spouses. It is therefore important to account for joint features in modeling retirement. This 
2:29:1473:14:14:72:546:546:12:Times:12:Times:C:paper  studies  a  structural collective  model  of  labor  supply  and  retirement  of  both  partners  in  a 
2:30:1583:14:14:72:545:532:12:Times:12:Times:C:couple  with  interdependent  preferences, imperfect  knowledge  of  preferences  of  the  spouse,  and 
2:31:1686:14:14:71:545:518:12:Times:12:Times:C:subjective expectations about the future. We propose a novel method to estimate preferences and 
2:32:1781:14:14:71:545:504:12:Times:12:Times:C:the  intra-household  bargaining  process,  which relies  on  stated  preferences  data  collected  in  the 
2:33:1888:14:14:72:546:490:12:Times:12:Times:C:Health and Retirement Study. Respondents were asked to choose between hypothetical retirement 
2:34:1981:14:14:72:546:476:12:Times:12:Times:C:trajectories  describing  the  retirement  ages  and  replacement rates  of  both  spouses  from  three 
2:35:2084:14:14:72:546:462:12:Times:12:Times:C:perspectives: considering their own preferences only, the preferences of their spouse only, or the 
2:36:2182:14:14:72:546:448:12:Times:12:Times:C:most  likely  decision  for  the  household.  With  these  data,  all  model  parameters are  identified  and 
2:37:2291:14:14:71:545:434:12:Times:12:Times:C:potential  sources  of  joint  retirement  can  be  disentangled.  We  find  that  males  misperceive their 
2:38:2398:14:14:71:545:420:12:Times:12:g_d0_f1:C:wives'  preferences,  overestimating  their  disutility  of  work.  Our  estimates  correct  for  this  bias. 
2:39:2507:14:14:71:545:406:12:Times:12:Times:C:They  suggest  that  correlation  in  unobserved  heterogeneity  components  of  the  partners'  marginal 
2:40:2613:14:14:71:545:392:12:Times:12:Times:C:utility of  leisure  explains  a  large  share  of  joint  retirement  decisions.  We  also  find  significant 
2:41:2723:14:28:71:524:378:12:Times:12:Times:C:positive complementarities in leisure, but this explains a much smaller part of joint retirement.
