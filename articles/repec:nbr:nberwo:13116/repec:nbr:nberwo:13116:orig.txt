2:24:1130:28:28:273:338:616:12:Times:12:Times:ABSTRACT
2:25:1138:28:14:72:540:588:12:Times:12:Times:The closing of the gender wage gap is an ongoing phenomenon in industrialized countries.  However,
2:26:1236:14:14:72:540:574:12:Times:12:Times:research has been limited in its ability to understand the causes of these changes, due in part to an
2:27:1337:14:14:72:540:560:12:Times:12:Times:inability to directly compare the work of women to that of men.  In this study, we use a new approach
2:28:1438:14:14:72:540:546:12:Times:12:Times:for analyzing changes in the gender pay gap that uses direct measures of job tasks and gives a comprehensive
2:29:1546:14:14:72:540:532:12:Times:12:Times:characterization of how work for men and women has changed in recent decades.  Using data from
2:30:1640:14:14:72:540:518:12:Times:12:Times:West Germany, we find that women have witnessed relative increases in non-routine analytic tasks
2:31:1736:14:14:72:540:504:12:Times:12:Times:and non-routine interactive tasks, which are associated with higher skill levels. The most notable difference
2:32:1845:14:14:72:540:490:12:Times:12:Times:between the genders is, however, the pronounced relative decline in routine task inputs among women
2:33:1944:14:14:72:540:476:12:Times:12:Times:with little change for men. These relative task changes explain a substantial fraction of the closing
2:34:2045:14:14:72:540:462:12:Times:12:Times:of the gender wage gap. Our evidence suggests that these task changes are driven, at least in part, by
2:35:2147:14:14:72:540:448:12:Times:12:Times:technological change. We also show that these task changes are related to the recent polarization of
2:36:2247:14:28:72:485:434:12:Times:12:Times:employment between low and high skilled occupations that we observed in the 1990s.

3:55:2845:27:28:90:525:626:144:Times:144:Times:tors  such  as  education  and  experience,  for  which  changes  have  been  more  favorable  for  
3:56:2945:28:28:90:522:598:144:Times:144:Times:women  than  for  men  in  recent  decades.  However,  a  substantial  portion  of  the  improve-
3:57:3042:28:27:90:526:570:144:Times:144:Times:ment in women’s labor market opportunities still remains unexplained.1  One reason for 
3:58:3129:27:28:90:525:543:144:Times:144:Times:this is that empirical research has been limited in its ability to directly compare women’s 
3:59:3221:28:27:90:191:515:144:Times:144:Times:work to that of men.

3:61:3336:28:28:90:525:460:144:Times:144:Times:gap that uses direct measures of job tasks and gives a comprehensive characterization of 
3:62:3425:28:27:90:525:432:144:Times:144:Times:how  work  for  men  and  women  has  changed  in  recent  decades.  The  strategy  is  based  on  
3:63:3524:27:28:90:522:405:144:Times:144:Times:the  task-based  framework  introduced  by  Autor,  Levy  and  Murnane  (2003).  The  advan-
3:64:3616:28:27:89:525:377:144:Times:144:Times:tage of this framework is that, in addition to the analysis of task changes, it also allows us 
3:65:3711:27:28:89:522:350:144:Times:144:Times:to investigate one of the potential underlying causes of changes in occupational skill re-
3:66:3801:28:27:89:522:322:144:Times:144:Times:quirements: workplace computerization. In this framework, the work performed in an oc-
3:67:3887:27:28:89:525:295:144:Times:144:Times:cupation is broken down into a series of tasks, each of which can be characterized based 
3:68:3976:28:28:89:525:267:144:Times:144:Times:on its substitutability or complementarity with computers.  Hence, it becomes predictable 
3:69:4066:28:27:89:467:239:144:Times:144:Times:how each occupation is likely to be affected by the introduction of computers. 
3:70:4145:27:28:125:526:212:144:Times:144:Times:Using  a  rich,  survey-based  data  set  from  West  Germany  covering  1979  to  1999,  
3:71:4235:28:27:89:521:184:144:Times:144:Times:we are able to measure skill requirements directly by using the task composition of occu-
3:72:4324:27:28:89:522:157:144:Times:144:Times:pations; that is, survey participants indicated the activities they perform on the job. Occu-
3:73:4417:28:28:89:522:129:144:Times:144:Times:pational skill requirements are characterized by five categories of tasks: non-routine ana-
3:74:4508:28:15:89:526:101:144:Times:144:Times:lytic (such as researching and analyzing), non-routine interactive (such as managing and

4:78:4817:27:28:90:513:681:144:Times:144:Times:(such as operating machinery) and non-routine manual (such as serving and repairing).

6:142:10125:27:28:90:525:295:144:Times:144:Times:plementary to both analytical and interactive non-routine cognitive tasks in the sense that

23:637:46235:28:27:126:526:515:144:Times:144:Times:There is one dimension along which the task-based framework diverges from the
