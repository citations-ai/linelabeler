2:29:1395:28:14:72:540:588:12:Times:12:Times:Property rights are commonly touted as a solution to common pool resource problems. But in practice
2:30:1494:14:14:72:540:574:12:Times:12:Times:the security of these property rights varies substantially owing to differences in design. In fisheries,
2:31:1598:14:14:72:540:560:12:Times:12:Times:the design of individual transferable quotas (ITQs) varies widely; the consequences of these design
2:32:1697:14:14:72:540:546:12:Times:12:Times:differences on economic outcomes has not been studied. To test whether the security of these property
2:33:1798:14:14:72:540:532:12:Times:12:Times:rights affects asset values, we compile a unique dataset to examine the relationship between the exclusivity
2:34:1906:14:14:72:540:518:12:Times:12:Times:of property rights and the dividend price ratios for ITQs. We find evidence that stronger property rights
2:35:2011:14:14:72:540:504:12:Times:12:Times:lead to higher asset values and lower dividend price ratios in ITQ fisheries. This pecuniary effect of
2:36:2113:14:14:72:540:490:12:Times:12:Times:property rights security informs the current policy debate on the design of property rights institutions
2:37:2217:14:42:72:224:476:12:Times:12:Times:for managing natural resources.
