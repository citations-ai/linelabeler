3:45:2186:0:23:85:174:754:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:1.  Introduction 
3:46:2201:23:23:106:513:731:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:The discussion on  the  sustainability of economic growth in  China’s  post-reform 
3:47:2283:23:24:85:513:708:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:economy  has  led  to studies on  China’s  productivity,  using either  growth  accounting  or 
3:48:2377:24:23:85:513:684:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:stochastic frontier analysis (SFA) (Chen et al., 2009; Chen et al., 2008; Wu, 2000, 2003, 
3:49:2466:23:23:85:516:661:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:2004; Hu and Khan, 1997; Woo, 1998; Mao and Koo, 1997; Borenstein and Ostry, 1996; 
3:50:2549:23:24:85:513:638:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Yang and  Lahr, 2010). For example, the studies  by Chow  and  Li (2002) and  Li (2003) 
3:51:2636:24:23:85:513:614:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:used  investment  figures  to  construct  capital  stock  to  estimate  China’s  national  total 
3:52:2733:23:24:85:513:591:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:factor productivity (TFP) growth rates have been extended by Liu and Li (2006) and Li 
3:53:2818:24:23:85:512:567:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:(2009) who incorporated  the  human  capital  variable and  alternative  investment  data to 
3:54:2910:23:23:85:513:544:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:examine  both  national  and  provincial  TFP  growth  rates.  Similar  studies  by  Wang  and 
3:55:3005:23:24:85:513:521:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Yao  (2003) have examined  the  sources  of  China’s economic growth, while Swamy 
3:56:3086:24:23:85:513:497:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:(2003),  Motohashi  (2007) and Bosworth  and  Collins  (2008) have compared China’s 
3:57:3169:23:24:85:254:474:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:TFP with other world economies.  
3:58:3200:24:23:106:513:450:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:In studying the  technical  change  in  the  United  States, Solow  (1957) differentiated 
3:59:3289:23:23:85:513:427:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:the  movement  along  the  production  function  caused  by input growth from shift  in  the 
3:60:3381:23:24:85:513:404:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:production  function  caused  by  technical  progress. Both Bauer  (1990)  and  Kumbhakar 
3:61:3471:24:23:85:512:380:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:and  Lovell (2000)  have  shown  that  TFP  growth composes of  technical  progress, 
3:62:3553:23:24:85:513:357:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:technical efficiency change and a scale economies effect. In theory, technical progress is 
3:63:3643:24:23:85:513:333:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:an  outward  shift  of  the  production  frontier and technical  efficiency  change  shows  the 
3:64:3738:23:23:85:512:310:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:movement  from  a  position  within  towards  a  position  on  the  production  frontier,  while 
3:65:3835:23:24:85:401:287:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:the scale economies effect reflects an increase in return to scale.  
3:66:3901:24:23:106:513:263:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Other   studies have   elaborated   and   extended   China’s   post-reform economic 
3:67:3984:23:24:85:512:240:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:productivity to efficiency analysis by using the Malmquist productivity index (MPI) and 
3:68:4072:24:23:85:513:216:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:data envelop analysis (DEA) (Wu, 1995, 2008). The MPI that decomposes productivity 
3:69:4152:23:23:85:513:193:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:into efficiency and technological  change has also been applied in Ma et al. (2002) and 
3:70:4240:23:24:85:513:170:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Movshuk  (2004).  Studies  on  the  productivity  and  efficiency  performance  of  individual 
3:71:4335:24:23:85:512:146:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:industries  in  China  have  been  conducted  by  Jefferson  (1990)  and Mu  and  Lee  (2005), 
3:72:4429:23:24:85:513:123:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:while  Yao et  al.  (2007)  and  Sun et  al.  (1999)  agreed  that SFA and  DEA  are  the  more 
3:73:4524:24:23:85:513:99:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:effective  approach  to  measure  the  technical efficiency of  industries. The SFA used  in 
3:74:4615:23:0:85:512:76:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:studies  on  the  China’s economy have provided useful  implications on the  production 
4:75:4700:0:24:85:513:755:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:function and technical efficiency performance (Huang and Kalirajan, 1998; Kalirajan et 
4:76:4786:24:23:85:513:731:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:al., 1996; Brummer et  al., 2006; Hu  and  McAleer, 2005; Tong, 1999; Wu 2000,  2003; 
4:77:4865:23:24:85:143:708:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Fu, 2005).  
4:78:4875:24:23:106:512:684:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Nonetheless,  studies on China’s post-reform economy have provided a  continued 
4:79:4953:23:23:85:513:661:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:debate  on  whether  technical  progress  or  technical  efficiency  is  the  more  important 
4:80:5047:23:24:85:512:638:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:contributing  factor  to China’s  TFP growth (Wu, 2000;  Li  and  Liu,  2011). After  more 
4:81:5136:24:23:85:513:614:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:than  three  decades  of  economic  reform  since  1978,  it  would  be  useful  to  examine  if 
4:82:5233:23:24:85:513:591:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:technical  efficiency  has  become  an  important  factor in China’s growth. In  addition,  an 
4:83:5326:24:23:85:512:567:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:objective measure on the technical efficiency among China’s provinces is crucial. Given 
4:84:5413:23:23:85:513:544:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:the extraordinary  nature, the  heterogeneity  of  development  in various regions  and 
4:85:5499:23:24:85:512:521:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:different time periods, a flexible stochastic frontier model can be used to study technical 
4:86:5589:24:23:85:314:497:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:efficiency in the post-reform China’s economy. 
4:87:5634:23:24:106:512:474:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Empirical  studies using the conventional stochastic  frontier  analysis  on  panel  data 
4:88:5723:24:23:85:513:450:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:models often implicitly impose a restriction that information differences have no effect 
4:89:5810:23:23:85:516:427:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:on the way risk-neutral decision makers utilize the same input bundle (Christopher et al., 
4:90:5896:23:24:85:513:404:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:2010).  The  result  is  that  informational  differences  are  mistaken  for differences  in 
4:91:5989:24:23:85:512:380:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:technical efficiency. The two specific effects that reflect information differences are the 
4:92:6079:23:24:85:512:357:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:individual effects and the time effects. They are  usually specified in stochastic frontier 
4:93:6171:24:23:85:513:333:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:models in the manner that individual effects are time-invariant and do not interact with 
4:94:6259:23:23:85:513:310:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:time effects, often in linearity or in parametric forms. However, when the individuals in 
4:95:6346:23:24:85:512:287:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:the  sample  differ  in technology and  efficiency  with  differenced  information,  especially 
4:96:6441:24:23:85:512:263:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:when such heterogeneity  changes  with  time,  the  linear  or  parametric  specification 
4:97:6530:23:24:85:513:240:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:cannot fully describe the heterogeneity in the production function and may induce a bias 
4:98:6617:24:23:85:513:216:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:in the measurement of technical efficiency. Conventional methods (either DEA or SFA) 
4:99:6701:23:23:85:513:193:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:attribute the  model  misspecification  errors to inefficiency  (Fu, 2005; Balaguer-Coll et 
4:100:6792:23:24:85:513:170:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:al., 2007; Grösche, 2009; Joseph et  al., 2010; Battese  and Coelli, 1992,  1995; 
4:101:6869:24:23:85:513:146:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Kumbhakar  and  Lovell, 2000;  Wu  2003). Researchers  have relaxed  distributional 
4:102:6951:23:24:85:513:123:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:assumptions in  the  error  component  and parametric  assumptions  in SFA to  achieve a 
4:103:7038:24:23:85:513:99:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:more  reliable  measurement  of  technical  efficiency (Greene, 2005; Kneip and Simar, 
4:104:7120:23:0:85:279:76:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:1996; and Henderson and Simar, 2005).

28:1026:57813:24:23:109:513:567:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:This  study uses  a  flexible stochastic production  function to  estimate technical 
28:1027:57896:23:23:85:513:544:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:efficiency   in China’s post-reform economy.   A   fully   nonparametric time-variant 
28:1028:57982:23:24:85:512:521:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:stochastic  frontier  model has  been  specified  to  allow for province  effects  and  time 
28:1029:58075:24:23:85:513:497:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:effects to enter  the  production  function  with  other inputs  in  a  nonparametric  way. The 
28:1030:58171:23:24:85:513:474:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:generalized kernel  estimation  is  applied  to  smooth  both  the  continuous  input  variables 
28:1031:58267:24:23:85:513:450:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:and the categorical unordered provinces and the ordered time periods. The flexibility in 
28:1032:58354:23:23:85:513:427:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:the production functional  form and  the  manner  of  individual  and  time  effects that 
28:1033:58444:23:24:85:513:404:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:entered into  the  production  model can  minimize  the  loss  in  the  measurement  of 
28:1034:58531:24:23:85:513:380:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:technical  efficiency, lead  to  robust  estimates of  the frontier  function and produce a 
28:1035:58621:23:24:85:513:357:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:reliable  measure  of  technical  efficiency. Model  specification  tests and  estimates  show 
28:1036:58714:24:23:85:513:333:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:that  the  fully  nonparametric  stochastic  model  is  more suitable  for  our  sample. The 
28:1037:58806:23:23:85:513:310:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:average  output  elasticity  of labor is  larger  than  those  of capital and  human  capital, 
28:1038:58900:23:24:85:194:287:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:which are all positive. 
28:1039:58923:24:23:106:513:263:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:The  measure  of  TE based  on  nonparametric  estimation shows  that the  average 
28:1040:59006:23:24:85:513:240:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:technical efficiency in  China has declined considerably  in the  mid-1980s,  but  has 
28:1041:59089:24:23:85:513:216:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:increased since 1992. Technical efficiency tends to be higher than the national average 
28:1042:59177:23:23:85:513:193:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:level in both Eastern and Southern regions. Although China has emphasized a lot on the 
28:1043:59260:23:24:85:513:170:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:development   of   the Western region,   its   technical   efficiency has remained low. 
28:1044:59346:24:23:85:513:146:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Unexpectedly,  Beijing,  being  the  capital  of  China, shows a  lower technical  efficiency 
28:1045:59438:23:24:85:221:123:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:than most other provinces.  
28:1046:59464:24:23:105:512:99:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:The  estimation  of  the  Tobit  model  of  technical  inefficiency  shows  that  the  time 
28:1047:59556:23:0:85:513:76:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:effects   of   technical   efficiency   in China’s post-reform economy   are   significantly 
29:1048:59650:0:24:85:513:755:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:contingent on the seven factors shown in Table 4. With  the  exception  of privatization, 
29:1049:59735:24:23:85:513:731:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:the  marginal  effects of all the other factors also  have  significant  time  effects.  There 
29:1050:59828:23:24:85:513:708:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:exists regional difference in technical efficiency. To maintain a sustainable, high-speed 
29:1051:59915:24:23:85:513:684:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:development,  China  should  continue  to  reform  and  adjust its  international  trade to  suit 
29:1052:60013:23:23:85:513:661:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:growth  and efficiency improvement. Foreign  direct  investments have  an  important  and 
29:1053:60100:23:24:85:513:638:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:large  positive  effect  on technical  efficiency, but it  should  be  further  encouraged  to 
29:1054:60195:24:23:85:512:614:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:neutralize the downward trend of the effect. China should attach great importance to its 
29:1055:60280:23:24:85:513:591:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:infrastructure  policy  and reappraise the infrastructure development policy  to  give  a 
29:1056:60368:24:23:85:270:567:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:positive effect on technical efficiency.
