3:56:3347:16:17:72:526:523:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:two  period  model  with  exogenous  labor  income,  the  equity  premium  is proportional  to  the

3:59:3643:16:17:72:229:474:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Asset Pricing Model (CCAPM). 
3:60:3671:17:16:86:526:457:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:In spite  of  the  theoretical simplicity  and  elegance  of  CAPM; when  faced  with  empirical

3:64:4051:16:16:72:526:392:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:CAPM  virtually  has  no  power  in explaining  cross-sectional  return  when  sorted  by  size  and 
3:65:4151:16:17:72:526:376:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:book-to-market  ratios.  Fama  and  French (1993) advocate  a  three  factor  model - market 
3:66:4242:17:16:72:523:359:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:return, the return of small less big stocks (SMB), and the return on a portfolio of high book-
3:67:4336:16:16:72:526:343:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:market value stocks less low book-market value stocks (HML). The Fama and French (1993) 
3:68:4422:16:16:72:526:327:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:model  is  a  resounding  success;  however,  it  is  still  not  clear  how  these  factors  relate  to

16:560:37637:33:16:86:525:359:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:An optimization-based regression model is estimated in this paper. We propose estimating 
16:561:37726:16:17:72:526:343:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:the  conditional  equity  premium  directly  by  conditional  consumption  and  market  return 
16:562:37821:17:15:72:526:326:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:volatilities.  Most  of  the  coefficient  signs  are  correct.  If 2R is  used  as  criteria,  the  volatility

16:564:38015:25:24:72:526:286:12:g_d0_f13:12:Times New Roman:Ludvigson’s (2002) scaled factor, outperforms other models. In various 2 tests, it is shown 
16:565:38106:24:17:72:526:262:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:that  the  theoretical  premium  renders  all  instrumental  variables  redundent,  providing support 
16:566:38208:17:16:72:165:245:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:for the BY model.  
16:567:38224:16:16:86:526:229:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:This  study  is  different  from  the  current research  on  the  Bansal  and  Yaron  (2004) models 
16:568:38323:16:17:72:525:213:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:that those  papers  attempt  to  estimate  some  of  the  parameters  in  the  general  equilibrium 
16:569:38423:17:16:72:526:196:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:model. For instance, Constantinides and Ghosh (2011) estimate the intertemporal elastcity of 
16:570:38514:16:16:72:526:180:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:substitution   from   the   Bansal   and   Yaron   (2004)   Euler   equation   using   aggregate U.S 
16:571:38615:16:16:72:526:164:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:consumption  and  dividend  growth  from  1931-2009.  The  original  Bansal  and  Yaron  (2004) 
16:572:38711:16:17:72:526:148:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:Euler equation is a function of two unobservable latent state variables. Through some form of 
16:573:38805:17:16:72:526:131:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:affine  transformation,  Constantinides  and  Ghosh  (2011) show  that the  Euler  equation is a 
16:574:38900:16:16:72:525:115:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:function of  observale  aggregate  log  price-dividend  ratio  and  log  risk  free  rate.  The  key 
16:575:39000:16:17:72:526:99:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:finding is that the Bansal and Yaron (2004) model requires higher consumption and dividend 
16:576:39091:17:0:72:526:82:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:growth persistence than the observed data. Bansal et al. (2012) found supporting evidence for 
17:577:39183:0:17:72:526:686:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:the  Long-Run  Risks    (LRR)  model – a  variant  of  Bansal  and  Yaron  (2004).  Using  annual 
17:578:39280:17:16:72:526:669:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:consumption and asset price data  from 1930-2008, the authors find that the first  and second 
17:579:39374:16:16:72:526:653:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:calibrated   moments   match   strongly   to   the actual   U.S   data;   even   the   lower   order 
17:580:39475:16:17:72:526:637:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:autocorrelations  fall  insider  the  confidence  band. However, Marakani  (2009) demonstrates 
17:581:39569:17:16:72:526:620:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:that, by variance ratios test, the LRR model implications are inconsistent with post-1929 U.S 
17:582:39663:16:16:72:526:604:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:data.  He  contends  that  early  studies  fail  to  address  the  time  aggregation  bias  inherent  in  the 
17:583:39773:16:17:72:526:588:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:data. Other related empirical studies include Colacito and Croce (2011), Hansen et al. (2008), 
17:584:39866:17:16:72:437:571:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:and Bansal et al. (2012). In essence, the above analyses are still calibration.

17:586:40045:16:16:72:526:539:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:depends  on  the  parameter  significance  and  coefficient  of variation.  In  order  to  faciliate 
17:587:40146:16:17:72:523:523:12:Times New Roman:12:Times New Roman:comparison with the existing studies, we are using the U.S data. An alternative to the Fama-
